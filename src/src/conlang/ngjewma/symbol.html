How to use: Type in character blocks, separate with a space. A character block is a consonant, approx., vowel, long marker, i.e.:
  • i, e, a, o, u, ɑ, ɒ are all blocks.
  • Make vowels long by doubling or use -.
  • Approxes are w, r, y. pi→pwi
  • Consonants: p b t d k g m n ŋ ɱ ɲ ɳ f h s z ɣ w r y -
  • Final consonants (their own character) are: p t k f s q n.
Their are some special characters:
  • ŋ - ng
  • ɳ - ngj
  • ɱ - mj
  • ɲ - nj
  • ɣ - gj
  • ɑ - à
  • ɒ - ò
